mLF: multivariate Loewner Framework

The mLF, for multivariate Loewner Framework, is introduced by A.C. Antoulas, I-V. Gosea and C. Poussot-Vassal in "On the Loewner framework, the Kolmogorov superposition theorem, and the curse of dimensionality", in SIAM Review (Research Spotlight), Vol. 67(4), pp. 737-770, November 2025. It allows constructing a $n$-variate rational function approximating a $n$-dimensional tensor (either real or complex valued). It is suited to approximate, from any $n$-dimensional tensor, $n$-variable static functions and $n$-variable (parametric) dynamical systems.
