mLF in a nutshell

Forewords

The code provided is given for open science purpose. Its principal objective is to accompany the contributions by the authors (listed in the reference page). It aims at being educative rather than industry-oriented. Evolutions (numerical improvements) may come with time. Please, cite the reference if used in your work and do not hesitate to contact us in case of bug or problem when using it, or even in case of success. Your case may also be included in the examples page.

For numerically more robust, scalable and versatile implementation, we invite readers and users to refer to the MDSPACK proposed library by MOR Digital Systems.

From tensor data to multivariate model

The main ingredient of the mLF is a data tensor. More specifically, it consists in the evaluation of the unknown function $f$, along the different values $(x_1,\cdots,x_n)$, thus leading to a $n$-dimensional tensor. $$ \left. \begin{array}{rcl} {\mathbf x_{1}} &=& \left[\begin{array}{cccc}x_{1}(1)&x_{1}(2)&\cdots&x_{1}(N_1)\end{array}\right]\\ {\mathbf x_{2}} &=& \left[\begin{array}{cccc}x_{2}(1)&x_{2}(2)&\cdots&x_{2}(N_2)\end{array}\right]\\ &\vdots& \\ {\mathbf x_{n}} &=& \left[\begin{array}{cccc}x_{n}(1)&x_{n}(2)&\cdots&x_{n}(N_n)\end{array}\right]\\ \end{array} \right\} \Rightarrow \mathcal T^{\otimes}_n $$


Illustation of a tensor data $T^{\otimes}_n$.

Based on this tensor set, we seek for a model of the form below:

$$ g(x_{1},\cdots,x_{n}) = \dfrac{\sum_{j_1=1}^{k_1}\cdots\sum_{j_n=1}^{k_n} \dfrac{c(j_1,\cdots,j_n)w(j_1,\cdots,j_n)}{(x_{1}-\lambda_{1}(j_1))\cdots(x_{n}-\lambda_{n}(j_n))}}{\sum_{j_1=1}^{k_1}\cdots\sum_{j_n=1}^{k_n} \dfrac{c(j_1,\cdots,j_n)}{(x_{1}-\lambda_{1}(j_1))\cdots(x_{n}-\lambda_{n}(j_n))}} $$ that approximates or discover the true unknown function $f$.
